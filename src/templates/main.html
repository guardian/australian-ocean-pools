Coastal heritage

A survey of ocean pools along the New South Wales coast

Sydney based architect Nicole Larkin has travelled up and down the coastline of NSW documenting theocean pools scattered along its varied shoreline. Guardian Australia has mapped all the pools she has recored on her seaboard wanderings.

Our journey begins in the Clarence Valley on the northern stretch of the New South Wales coast at the town of Yamba. From there we head southward eventually making our way to the Bega Valley Shire some 1000km away. All of the 59 pools that we have presented here are still actively in use today. Many were built as public works projects during the depression. Most were ingeniously constructed by exploiting some natural topographic feature of the local area. All of them have become a focal point within their coastal communities.
